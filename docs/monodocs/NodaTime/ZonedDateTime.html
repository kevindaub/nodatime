ZonedDateTime Struct

A date and time with an associated chronology - or to look at it a different way, a LocalDateTime plus a time zone.

public struct ZonedDateTime : IEquatable<ZonedDateTime>

⊟ Remarks

Some seemingly valid values do not represent a valid instant due to the local time moving forward during a daylight saving transition, thus "skipping" the given value. Other values occur twice (due to local time moving backward), in which case a ZonedDateTime will always represent the later of the two possible times, when converting it to an instant.

A value constructed with "new ZonedDateTime()" will represent the Unix epoch in the ISO calendar system in the UTC time zone. That is the only situation in which the chronology is assumed rather than specified.
